From the Kitchens
of the Nizams

Hyderabadi Dum Biryani traces its roots to the royal kitchens of the Nizams of Hyderabad. Crafted with patience, aromatic spices, and slow dum cooking, this dish became a symbol of royal hospitality and culinary mastery.

Each grain of rice absorbs the essence of carefully blended spices, creating a dish that is both fragrant and unforgettable. We honor this ancient tradition in every pot we prepare.

The Ritual of Preparation

1

Marination

Premium cuts of meat are rested for 12 hours in a secret yogurt-based spice blend.

2

Layering

Parboiled long-grain Basmati is layered over the meat with fresh herbs and fried onions.

3

Sealing

The Handi is hermetically sealed with fresh dough, creating a pressure-cook environment.

4

Slow Cooking

The pot sits on a bed of warm embers, slowly transforming into royal perfection.
